# Union National Tax — Website PRD

## Document Purpose
This PRD defines the strategy, structure, content priorities, conversion goals, and implementation requirements for the next version of the Union National Tax website.

This website is not meant to function like a generic tax-prep brochure site. It should position Union National Tax as a strategy-first boutique tax and financial advisory firm for growth-minded business owners, with a public-facing emphasis on S-Corp strategy, proactive tax planning, and Fractional CFO support.

---

## 1. Project Summary

### Project Name
Union National Tax Website Repositioning and Conversion Upgrade

### Business Context
Union National Tax already offers a strong mix of services, including:
- Fractional CFO Services
- Tax Planning Consulting
- Strategic Bookkeeping
- Tax Filing & Preparation
- New Business Formation
- S-Corp Tax Advantage Program
- Construction CFO Partnership
- Restaurant CFO Partnership

The problem is not lack of services. The problem is how those services are framed, prioritized, grouped, and converted on the website.

The website must shift the public perception of the firm away from commodity tax prep and toward high-trust strategic advisory.

### Strategic Position
The site should reinforce these ideas consistently:
- many business owners are legally overpaying in taxes
- entity structure can materially affect take-home income
- year-round planning beats reactive filing
- better books and reporting lead to better business decisions
- Union National Tax is an ongoing advisor, not just a filing vendor

---

## 2. Business Goals

### Primary Goals
1. Increase qualified strategy-call bookings.
2. Increase inquiries for the S-Corp Tax Advantage Program.
3. Position Fractional CFO and Tax Planning as premium advisory services.
4. Improve perceived specialization and trust.
5. Create a cleaner service architecture that supports future content, funnels, and vertical landing pages.

### Secondary Goals
1. Support qualification of better-fit clients.
2. Reduce low-fit “just file my taxes” leads.
3. Build stronger trust through clarity, specificity, and service packaging.
4. Create a scalable foundation for industry pages and future campaigns.

### Non-Goals
- The site is not intended to be a generic firm directory.
- The site is not intended to act as a tax-debt relief funnel.
- The site is not intended to sell low-cost commodity tax filing as the lead offer.

---

## 3. Target Audiences

### Primary Audience
Owner-led small businesses that are profitable or growing and likely need more strategy, better structure, and clearer numbers.

### Priority Audience Segments
1. Construction / trades businesses
2. Restaurant operators
3. Real estate operators / investors
4. E-commerce founders

### Shared Audience Traits
- suspects they are overpaying in taxes
- wants more than basic filing
- may have an outdated or inefficient entity setup
- values clarity, responsiveness, and plain-English advice
- is open to recurring advisory support if the value is clear

### Lower-Priority Audience
Simple one-time tax prep shoppers with low strategic need.

---

## 4. Core Value Proposition

### Primary Positioning Statement
Union National Tax helps business owners reduce taxes legally, optimize entity structure, and gain clearer financial control through proactive planning, S-Corp strategy, and ongoing advisory support.

### Primary Promise
Stop overpaying the IRS. Build a smarter business.

### Website-Level Messaging Priorities
The website should repeatedly communicate:
- you may be overpaying because of how your business is structured
- filing alone is not a tax strategy
- better reporting leads to better decisions
- growth-minded owners need year-round financial guidance
- Union National Tax offers both strategy and implementation support

### Language to Avoid
Avoid leading with language that makes the brand sound like:
- a generic tax relief firm
- a low-cost tax prep provider
- a “fast refund” shop
- a fear-first IRS rescue brand

Avoid emphasizing first:
- settlement
- rescue
- instant resolution
- refund-driven language
- price-first commodity positioning

## 9. Content and Copy Requirements

### Voice and Tone
The copy should feel:
- strategic
- clear
- plain-English
- confident
- high-trust
- owner-minded
- premium but approachable

### Copy Rules
- lead with outcomes, not tasks
- emphasize clarity, structure, planning, and control
- avoid sounding salesy, gimmicky, or fear-driven
- avoid jargon overload
- avoid tax-relief-style urgency language

### Proof Requirements
The site should make room for:
- credential markers
- audit protection details where relevant
- industry expertise framing
- process clarity
- testimonial / case study modules if available
- “who this is for” and “who this is not for” style clarifiers

## 16. Success Criteria

The redesign is successful if:
1. the site clearly feels strategy-first, not prep-first
2. the S-Corp offer becomes easy to understand and easy to act on
3. the advisory services feel premium and differentiated
4. the site drives more qualified strategy-call behavior
5. services are grouped and prioritized logically
6. the site creates a strong foundation for future funnel and content work

---

## 17. Key Strategic Decisions Locked by This PRD

1. Union National Tax is positioned as a strategic advisory-led firm.
2. The S-Corp Tax Advantage Program is the primary front-end wedge.
3. Fractional CFO and Tax Planning are premium service anchors.
4. Construction and Restaurant verticals are active industry-specific offers.
5. Bookkeeping, filing, and formation remain important but supporting.
6. The primary CTA is strategy-call driven, not generic contact-driven.
7. The website should qualify for fit, not maximize raw lead volume at any cost.
