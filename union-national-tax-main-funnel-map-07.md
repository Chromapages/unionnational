# Union National Tax — Main Funnel Map

## Document Purpose

This document defines the **primary lead-generation and conversion funnel** for Union National Tax.

Its purpose is to ensure the website, intake flow, booking flow, CRM setup, and follow-up logic all support the same business goal:

**Convert qualified small business owners into booked tax strategy calls and then into advisory clients.**

This funnel is intentionally designed to reflect Union National Tax’s approved market position:

- strategy-first, not generic tax prep
- S-Corp and entity optimization as the sharp front-end wedge
- proactive tax planning and Fractional CFO services as premium follow-on offers
- industry relevance for Construction and Restaurants, while remaining open to other qualified small business owners

---

## Primary Funnel Objective

The main funnel should generate:

- qualified assessment submissions
- qualified strategy call bookings
- higher-fit advisory opportunities
- smoother progression from interest to proposal

The funnel should **not** behave like a generic “contact us” path.

The website is not being optimized for broad inquiry volume.
It is being optimized for **qualified business-owner conversations**.

---

## Core Funnel Strategy

### Primary Funnel

**Traffic → Homepage / Service / Industry page → Primary CTA → Multi-step intake / assessment → Qualification → Strategy call booking → Call completed → Proposal / advisory recommendation → Onboarding**

This is the main business-development path the site should be built around.

### Strategic Logic Behind the Funnel

Cold traffic is more likely to respond to a clear value wedge than to a vague advisory pitch.

Because of that, the main funnel should lead with one of two entry angles:

1. **See If an S-Corp Could Save You Money**
2. **Book a Tax Strategy Call**

The first angle is best for colder traffic and top-of-funnel visitors.
The second angle is best for warmer traffic and high-intent visitors.

## Primary Conversion Paths

There should be **three approved conversion paths**.

## Path A — Main Cold-Traffic Path

**Visitor lands on homepage / S-Corp page → clicks S-Corp CTA → completes assessment → gets qualified → books strategy call**

### Purpose
This is the primary acquisition path for most first-time visitors.

### Best-fit visitor
- self-employed business owner
- LLC or sole proprietor
- profitable operator likely overpaying in taxes
- owner open to entity optimization and advisory help

### Why this path matters
It gives users a concrete next step without requiring them to understand every service first.

---

## Path B — High-Intent Strategy Path

**Visitor lands on service / industry / about page → clicks Book a Tax Strategy Call → completes qualification form → books directly**

### Purpose
This path is for warmer visitors who do not need as much education.

### Best-fit visitor
- referred lead
- branded search traffic
- business owner already looking for strategic help
- higher-revenue operator with immediate interest

---

## Path C — Low-Intent / Not-Ready Path

**Visitor explores site → does not book → enters nurture through soft conversion**

### Soft conversions may include
- submit intake without booking
- request more info
- download future resource
- join email list

### Purpose
Prevent qualified but not-ready traffic from disappearing.

## Intake / Assessment Flow

The intake step should sit between curiosity and booking for most visitors.

### Recommended intake model
A **multi-step assessment form** that qualifies the lead before or during booking.

### Core goals of the form
- improve lead quality
- route users toward the right service path
- gather useful sales context before the call
- reduce unqualified bookings
- personalize follow-up later

### Recommended form length
5 to 8 short steps.

### Recommended fields

#### Step 1 — Business basics
- first name
- last name
- email
- phone
- company name
- website (optional)

#### Step 2 — Business profile
- industry
- business type
- state
- number of years in business
- estimated annual revenue range

#### Step 3 — Entity and tax structure
- current entity type
- whether they are already taxed as an S-Corp
- whether they have an accountant or bookkeeper now
- whether books are current

#### Step 4 — Needs and interest
- what they need help with
  - S-Corp evaluation
  - tax planning
  - fractional CFO
  - bookkeeping
  - tax filing
  - business formation
- biggest current pain point

#### Step 5 — Qualification signal
- urgency / timeframe
- willingness to invest in professional support
- preferred next step

#### Step 6 — Booking handoff
If qualified, direct to booking page.  
If not qualified, send to softer nurture or “we’ll review and follow up” path.

## Qualification Logic

The funnel should qualify leads before sending too many weak bookings into the calendar.

### Strong qualification indicators
- owner-led small business
- profitable or growing business
- revenue typically above early startup level
- currently self-employed, LLC, or unsure about structure
- interested in tax savings, planning, financial clarity, or advisory
- located in supported service area / acceptable operating footprint
- serious about help in the next 30–90 days

### Medium qualification indicators
- newer business but growing quickly
- not yet ideal revenue level but likely to become a fit
- books are somewhat messy but owner wants support

### Weak qualification indicators
- only wants cheapest tax filing
- no business entity or unclear business activity
- purely one-time low-budget need
- no readiness to engage professionally

### Qualification routing

#### Qualified
Send directly to strategy-call booking.

#### Borderline
Collect info and send to manual review or lower-friction follow-up.

#### Unqualified
Do not push into the calendar. Route to contact response, lighter education, or disqualify politely.

## Booking Flow

### Booking page purpose
Turn qualified leads into calendar bookings with minimal friction.

### Booking page should include
- strong headline
- one clear explanation of who the call is for
- what the call covers
- who should not book
- meeting length
- what to prepare
- calendar embed or external scheduling flow
- confirmation expectation

### Recommended booking-page positioning
This call is not a generic consultation.
It is a **Tax Strategy Call** for business owners who want to reduce tax waste, improve structure, and gain financial clarity.

### Booking-page copy should clarify
- this is for business owners, not personal tax questions
- the call is strategic, not a free advice session for everyone
- the goal is to determine fit and next best recommendation

---

## Confirmation and Thank-You Flow

Once a booking is completed, the lead should see a confirmation page.

### Confirmation page goals
- reduce no-shows
- reinforce professionalism
- set expectations
- gather missing details if needed

### Confirmation page should include
- booking confirmed message
- date and time reminder
- what they should prepare
- documents or details to bring
- what happens after the call
- contact option if they need to reschedule

### Optional post-booking enhancement
Short pre-call questionnaire for:
- top financial concerns
- current entity details
- current provider situation
- key goals for the next 12 months

---

## No-Show Recovery Flow

No-show handling should be part of the funnel map, not an afterthought.

### If lead books but does not attend
Trigger:
- no-show email within same day
- rebooking link
- reminder of why the call matters
- optional short SMS if permitted

### If lead reschedules
Keep CRM stage active and preserve all intake information.

### If lead ignores follow-up
Move to nurture after follow-up threshold is reached.

---

## Proposal Path After the Call

The funnel does not end at the booking.
The post-call path should be defined.

### After a qualified strategy call
The advisor should place the lead into one of these recommendation paths:

#### Path 1 — S-Corp Tax Advantage Program
Best for owner-operators who are structured incorrectly or likely overpaying.

#### Path 2 — Tax Planning Consulting
Best for owners needing year-round planning but not full CFO support.

#### Path 3 — Fractional CFO Services
Best for more mature or higher-growth businesses that need forecasting, financial visibility, and deeper strategy.

#### Path 4 — Strategic Bookkeeping + Filing support
Best for clients who need operational cleanup before more advanced advisory.

#### Path 5 — New Business Formation
Best for early-stage operators who need entity setup first.

### Proposal goal
Move the lead into the right advisory relationship, not just the highest-ticket offer.

---

## Nurture Path

Not every qualified visitor will book immediately.
A nurture layer should exist even in the initial funnel version.

### Leads who should enter nurture
- started but did not finish intake
- submitted intake but did not book
- booked and went cold
- had a call but were not ready
- referral leads not ready yet

### Early nurture topics
- signs you may be overpaying in taxes
- when an S-Corp makes sense
- mistakes owners make by waiting until year-end
- why clean books matter for tax strategy
- when CFO support becomes valuable

### Nurture objective
Keep Union National Tax positioned as the strategic option until the lead is ready.

## Automation Triggers

Even if full automation is built later, the funnel map should define the intended triggers now.

### Recommended trigger set

#### Trigger 1 — Form started
Optional reminder if abandoned.

#### Trigger 2 — Intake submitted
- tag lead by service interest
- assign qualification status
- send confirmation email
- route to booking if qualified

#### Trigger 3 — Strategy call booked
- send confirmation email
- send calendar reminder
- notify internal owner

#### Trigger 4 — Strategy call no-show
- send rebooking follow-up
- move status to no-show / nurture review

#### Trigger 5 — Proposal sent
- start proposal follow-up sequence

#### Trigger 6 — Closed won
- trigger onboarding workflow

#### Trigger 7 — Closed lost or not ready
- move to nurture path

## Funnel Messaging Principles

The funnel should consistently reinforce these ideas:

- you may be overpaying because your business is structured wrong
- tax strategy should be proactive, not rushed at year-end
- better books and reporting lead to better tax and business decisions
- Union National Tax is a strategic partner, not just a filing service
- the call is designed for business owners who want real financial clarity

### Messaging to avoid in funnel copy
- generic “free consultation” language
- tax relief tone
- debt/fear-first framing unless it is specifically relevant
- mass-market low-cost tax prep language
- vague “contact us to learn more” copy with no strategic framing

---

## Final Recommendation

The main Union National Tax funnel should be built around this exact path:

**Homepage / S-Corp / Industry page → strategy-led CTA → qualification form / assessment → Tax Strategy Call → offer recommendation → proposal → onboarding**

This is the cleanest funnel because it aligns with:
- the approved brand positioning
- the service hierarchy
- the strongest front-end wedge
- the premium advisory direction of the business

It also creates a system that can later support:
- vertical landing pages
- paid traffic campaigns
- email nurture
- referral flows
- reporting and optimization
