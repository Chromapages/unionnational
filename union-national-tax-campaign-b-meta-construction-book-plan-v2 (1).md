# Union National Tax — Campaign B Plan v2  
## Meta Construction Book + Profit-Control Funnel

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

**Campaign Name:** Campaign B — Construction Profit-Control Funnel  
**Primary Platform:** Meta: Facebook + Instagram  
**Primary Authority Asset:** *The Money-Making Blueprint for Construction Companies*  
**Primary Diagnostic Asset:** Construction Profitability Assessment  
**Final Conversion Offer:** Construction CFO Strategy Call  
**Core Service Path:** Construction CFO Partnership  

Campaign B is a Meta-first acquisition funnel for construction business owners. The campaign should not behave like a generic book download campaign.

It should be positioned as a **contractor profit-control funnel** that uses the book as the authority asset, then moves leads into a diagnostic assessment and finally into a Construction CFO Strategy Call.

---

## 2. Strategic Position

Campaign B should speak directly to contractor owners who are busy, growing, stressed, and unclear on where profit is going.

The core contractor mindset:

- “We’re busy, but the profit isn’t there.”
- “The jobs always seem to run over.”
- “Cash flow is killing us.”
- “I feel like I’m working nonstop, but the business still feels chaotic.”
- “We can’t seem to grow without everything falling apart.”

### Strategic Thesis

**The book builds authority.**  
**The assessment exposes the profit leaks.**  
**The strategy call converts qualified contractors into advisory clients.**  
**GHL handles automation, scoring, routing, nurture, booking, and pipeline movement.**

---

## 3. Core Campaign Message

**Busy jobs do not always mean profitable jobs.**

### Stronger Hook

**If your construction company is busy but profit still feels unpredictable, the problem is probably not the work. It is the system.**

### Campaign Promise

Help construction owners understand where profit is being lost through weak job costing, cash flow gaps, estimating problems, change order issues, margin fade, and lack of operational financial control.

---

## 4. Key Campaign Correction

The campaign should not be:

```text
Meta Ad → Download Book → Generic Nurture
```

It should be:

```text
Meta Ad
→ Construction Book / Profit-Control Blueprint
→ Construction-Specific Nurture
→ Construction Profitability Assessment
→ Construction CFO Strategy Call
→ Construction CFO Partnership
```

The book is not the destination.  
The book is the trust builder.

---

## 5. Proof Strategy

Campaign B needs proof, but the proof must be accurate and defensible.

Do not invent:

- contractor result claims
- contractor counts
- “90% of contractors” statistics
- exact margin improvement numbers
- client success metrics not documented

### Proof Type 1 — Authority Proof

Use Jason’s actual credibility:

**Jason Astwood, EA, FSCP®, LUTCF®**  
Founder, Union National Tax  
Tax strategist and Fractional CFO advisor for business owners

Construction relevance:

**Helping business owners move from reactive financial chaos to clearer job costing, cash flow control, and profit visibility.**

### Proof Type 2 — Framework Proof

Show the contractor control system:

- Job Costing
- Estimating Discipline
- Cash Flow Forecasting
- Margin Control
- Change Order Control
- Weekly Financial Rhythm

### Proof Type 3 — Example Math

Use scenario-based examples instead of fabricated testimonials.

Example:

**A contractor doing $1.2M/year with just 3% margin fade could be giving up $36,000 in profit visibility before year-end.**

Phrase carefully:

- “could be”
- “example”
- “scenario”
- “potentially”

### Proof Type 4 — Real Case Studies, If Available

If Union National Tax has real contractor results, add them later as verified proof blocks.

Example format:

```text
Contractor Case Study:
Before: unclear job costing, inconsistent cash flow, reactive pricing
After: weekly job-cost review rhythm, clearer margin visibility, stronger billing discipline
Result: [insert verified metric only]
```

## 16. Construction Profitability Assessment Questions

## Section 1 — Business Profile

- business name
- trade type
- annual revenue band
- employee count
- number of active jobs per month

---

## Section 2 — Job Costing

- Do you track job costs by project?
- Do you compare estimated vs. actual costs weekly?
- Do you track labor, materials, equipment, and subs separately?
- Do you know which jobs make or lose money?

---

## Section 3 — Estimating

- Do your estimates use current labor and production data?
- Do you calculate overhead recovery?
- Do jobs often finish below expected margin?
- Do you track margin fade?

---

## Section 4 — Cash Flow

- Do you use 90-day cash flow forecasts?
- Do you bill by schedule or milestone?
- Do you have retainage issues?
- Do you ever struggle with payroll despite being busy?

---

## Section 5 — Operational Control

- Are change orders captured and approved before work continues?
- Are subs held accountable to schedule and scope?
- Do you have weekly project financial reviews?
- Do you have documented production systems?

---

## Section 6 — Intent

- What is the biggest issue right now?
- How soon do you want help?
- Are you open to a construction financial strategy call?

## 25. Prompt for GHL AI — Construction Book Workflow

```text
Create a GoHighLevel workflow named “LEAD — Construction Book Downloaded — Deliver & Nurture”.

Trigger:
Form submitted or Meta lead form submitted where the form name is “The Money-Making Blueprint for Construction Companies”.

Workflow purpose:
When a contractor downloads the construction book from a Meta ad, create or update the contact, tag the lead, deliver the book, create a lead qualification opportunity, and start a construction-specific nurture sequence that moves the lead toward the Construction Profitability Assessment and then a Construction CFO Strategy Call.

4. Set custom field niche_vertical to CONSTRUCTION.
```

- tracking issues

- CTR
- CPC
- landing page view rate
- form starts
- early lead quality
- comments and message quality

Pause ads with:

- weak CTR
- high CPC compared to other ads
- no form starts
- low-quality leads
- misleading comments
- too many non-owner leads

Scale ads with:

- contractor owner leads
- strong book download rate

- strong revenue bands

Shift attention from cost per book download to:

- cost per assessment completion
