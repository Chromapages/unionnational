# Union National Tax — Brand Positioning Summary

## Core Positioning

Union National Tax is **not** a tax relief company and should avoid sounding like one.

The real positioning is:

**A boutique tax strategy and financial advisory firm for small business owners who want to reduce taxes, improve cash flow, and build long-term wealth.**

## What This Clearly Says About the Brand

### 1. They are strategy-first, not return-first
They should not lead with “we file taxes.”
They should lead with **tax reduction, entity structure, CFO guidance, and proactive planning**.

### 2. Their strongest wedge is the S-Corp angle
That is a clear, concrete, high-value entry point.

**“Are you overpaying because your business is set up wrong?”** is a much stronger hook than generic tax messaging.

### 3. Their real value is ongoing advisory
Fractional CFO services changes the game. It means they are not just seasonal preparers. They are an **ongoing financial partner**.

### 4. Their niche focus matters
Construction, real estate, and e-commerce are strong verticals because they each have:

- tax complexity
- cash flow issues
- owner-operator decision fatigue
- major upside from better entity structure and planning

The firm should feel:

- specialized
- high-trust
- business-owner focused
- proactive
- financially strategic

Not:

- mass market
- generic
- urgent tax debt relief
- “fast refund” style
- low-cost commodity prep

## Refined Brand Position

**Union National Tax helps small business owners legally reduce their tax burden, optimize entity structure, and gain clearer financial control through proactive tax strategy, S-Corp planning, and fractional CFO support.**

## Best Core Message

A strong plain-English message:

**Stop overpaying the IRS. Build a smarter business.**

This works because it connects:

- pain: overpaying taxes
- desire: smarter growth
- value: strategy, not just filing

## Best Service Hierarchy

### Primary Offers
- S-Corp tax strategy and entity optimization
- Fractional CFO services
- Proactive business tax planning

### Supporting Offers
- Tax preparation and compliance
- Bookkeeping and monthly financial reporting
- IRS representation

That order matters. It makes them look premium and strategic instead of transactional.

## Best-Fit Client Profile

Their best client is typically:

- owner-led small business
- profitable or growing
- likely paying too much in taxes
- needs better structure and cleaner numbers
- wants guidance, not just forms filed

Especially:

- HVAC owners
- general contractors
- real estate operators and investors
- e-commerce founders

## What the Website and Messaging Should Emphasize

The site should repeatedly communicate these ideas:

- You may be overpaying in taxes
- Your entity structure may be costing you money
- You need year-round strategy, not year-end scrambling
- Better reporting leads to better decisions
- Tax planning should support wealth building, not just compliance

## What to Avoid in the Messaging

Avoid words and framing that make them sound like a generic relief firm, such as:

- settlement
- tax rescue
- national relief
- instant resolution
- debt-first fear messaging

Unless that is a real service line they want to push, it weakens the premium advisory position.

## Strategic Takeaway

Union National Tax should be positioned as:

**The tax strategist and financial clarity partner for growth-minded business owners.**

That is much stronger than:

- tax prep firm
- bookkeeping office
- accountant for everyone

The most powerful angle is:

**“We help business owners stop overpaying, structure correctly, and run financially smarter companies.”**
