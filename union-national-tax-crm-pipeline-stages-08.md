# Union National Tax — CRM Pipeline Stages (Step 8)

## Purpose

This document defines the CRM pipeline for Union National Tax so every inbound lead, booked call, proposal, and follow-up moves through a consistent operating model.

The pipeline is designed to support the approved brand and funnel direction:

- strategy-first positioning
- S-Corp / tax-savings wedge at the front end
- premium advisory path into Tax Planning, Fractional CFO, and industry-specific partnerships
- compliance and bookkeeping offers as supporting services rather than the primary acquisition identity

This CRM structure should mirror the website funnel, intake flow, booking flow, and sales handoff process.

---

## Primary Objectives

The CRM should help the team:

- qualify and prioritize the right leads
- separate advisory-fit leads from commodity tax-prep inquiries
- create a consistent follow-up cadence
- track which offers are generating qualified opportunities
- reduce lead leakage between form submission and booked strategy call
- create clean handoff points from sales into onboarding and delivery

---

## Pipeline Overview

### Recommended Primary Pipeline Stages

1. New Lead
2. Unqualified
3. Qualified
4. Intake Submitted
5. Strategy Call Booked
6. Strategy Call Completed
7. Proposal / Recommendation Sent
8. Closed Won
9. Closed Lost
10. Nurture

This is the minimum viable pipeline for Phase 1.

---

## Stage Definitions

## 1) New Lead

### Definition
A lead has entered the system through the website, referral, paid traffic, direct inquiry, or manual entry, but has not yet been reviewed.

### Common Sources
- homepage CTA
- S-Corp landing page
- service pages
- contact page
- referral partner
- direct call or email
- manual import

### Required Actions
- assign source
- create owner
- capture timestamp
- check for duplicates
- trigger first-touch acknowledgement

### Exit Criteria
Move the lead to:
- **Unqualified** if clearly not a fit
- **Qualified** if enough information is present to confirm fit
- **Intake Submitted** if they completed the assessment/intake form immediately

---

## 2) Unqualified

### Definition
The lead is not a fit for the current business model, is outside the target client range, is seeking the wrong service type, or is spam.

### Common Reasons
- only wants low-cost tax filing
- individual consumer return with no advisory potential
- outside service geography or business scope if applicable
- not a business owner
- business too early / too small for advisory fit
- spam / duplicate / invalid submission
- not interested in consultation or strategic engagement

### Required Actions
- apply unqualified reason
- remove from active sales follow-up
- optionally send polite redirect or alternate service response

### Exit Criteria
This is typically a terminal stage unless the lead returns later as a better fit.

---

## 3) Qualified

### Definition
The lead appears to match the client profile and service mix, but full intake may still be incomplete.

### Qualified Lead Indicators
A qualified lead typically has several of the following:

- owner-led business
- business revenue or trajectory suggests advisory potential
- likely overpaying or structurally inefficient
- interest in S-Corp, tax planning, bookkeeping, or CFO-level support
- business complexity beyond one-off filing
- willingness to speak with a strategist / advisor

### Required Actions
- verify service interest
- verify business type / industry
- request or route to intake form if not already completed
- tag likely offer path

### Exit Criteria
Move to:
- **Intake Submitted** after the form/assessment is completed
- **Unqualified** if later review shows bad fit
- **Strategy Call Booked** if the booking happened before intake completion and the process allows it

---

## 4) Intake Submitted

### Definition
The lead has completed the main qualification form, assessment, or strategy intake.

### Required Intake Data
- full name
- business name
- email
- phone
- service interest
- industry
- annual revenue range
- current entity type
- bookkeeping status
- primary pain point
- desired outcome
- urgency / timeline
- referral source or acquisition source

### Required Actions
- review responses
- assign lead score
- confirm fit for strategy call
- determine provisional offer path
- trigger booking prompt if not already booked

### Exit Criteria
Move to:
- **Strategy Call Booked** once the consultation is scheduled
- **Unqualified** if intake shows poor fit
- **Nurture** if potentially fit later but not ready now

---

## 5) Strategy Call Booked

### Definition
The lead has scheduled a consultation, tax strategy call, or S-Corp review call.

### Required Actions
- send confirmation email
- send calendar invite
- send reminder sequence
- prepare pre-call notes
- route intake summary to assigned advisor

### Internal Pre-Call Prep Should Include
- source
- service interest
- likely fit level
- entity type
- revenue range
- key pain points
- likely recommendation path

### Exit Criteria
Move to:
- **Strategy Call Completed** after the meeting occurs
- remain in this stage with no-show logic until rescheduled or marked lost

---

## 6) Strategy Call Completed

### Definition
The consultation happened and the advisor has enough information to make a recommendation.

### Required Actions
- log meeting notes
- log offer recommendation
- log objections / concerns
- log next action
- determine whether proposal is needed

### Recommendation Categories
- S-Corp Tax Advantage Program
- Tax Planning Consulting
- Fractional CFO Services
- Strategic Bookkeeping
- New Business Formation
- Construction CFO Partnership
- Restaurant CFO Partnership
- not a fit / nurture

### Exit Criteria
Move to:
- **Proposal / Recommendation Sent** if formal next step is delivered
- **Closed Won** if accepted on call and no proposal needed
- **Nurture** if interested but not ready
- **Closed Lost** if the opportunity is dead

---

## 7) Proposal / Recommendation Sent

### Definition
A written recommendation, service proposal, pricing path, or next-step scope has been delivered.

### Required Actions
- log send date
- log recommended package / service mix
- trigger follow-up cadence
- track open / reply / meeting if available

### Proposal Types
- single-service proposal
- tiered advisory recommendation
- S-Corp + bookkeeping + planning bundle
- CFO partnership proposal

### Exit Criteria
Move to:
- **Closed Won** when accepted
- **Closed Lost** when declined
- **Nurture** if timing is the issue and future follow-up is appropriate

---

## 8) Closed Won

### Definition
The lead has agreed to move forward and is now a client.

### Required Actions
- mark closed date
- mark closed value
- mark primary service sold
- trigger onboarding workflow
- assign delivery owner
- move record into client management pipeline if one exists

### Required Captures
- won service/package
- projected annual value
- source attribution
- industry
- close notes

### Exit Criteria
Terminal stage in the sales pipeline.

---

## 9) Closed Lost

### Definition
The opportunity is not moving forward and should no longer stay in active sales follow-up.

### Common Lost Reasons
- price objection
- no urgency
- chose competitor
- wanted basic filing only
- not enough complexity / not enough fit
- poor timing
- unresponsive after proposal
- internal hold / decision delayed too long

### Required Actions
- select lost reason
- log notes
- decide whether to place in nurture

### Exit Criteria
Terminal stage unless reactivated later.

---

## 10) Nurture

### Definition
The lead is not ready to buy now, but is relevant enough to keep in periodic follow-up.

### Examples
- interested after tax season
- waiting for revenue milestone
- needs cleanup before advisory work
- not ready to switch providers yet
- wants more education first

### Required Actions
- tag nurture reason
- assign nurture segment
- add to email sequence or manual follow-up cadence
- set next review date

### Exit Criteria
Move back to:
- **Qualified** when interest reactivates
- **Intake Submitted** if they complete a new assessment
- **Strategy Call Booked** if they re-engage directly

## Qualification Framework

## High-Fit Lead Signals

Prioritize leads that match several of the following:

- business owner, not just individual filer
- revenue-producing company with meaningful tax exposure
- likely candidate for S-Corp strategy or entity optimization
- active need for bookkeeping, planning, or CFO-level insight
- industry fit such as construction or restaurants, with room to expand later
- values proactive planning, not just deadline filing
- willing to invest in strategic support

## Lower-Fit Lead Signals

- one-time basic return seeker
- refund-driven shopper
- very low-budget inquiry
- not yet operating a real business
- no appetite for strategy or advisory work

## Lead Scoring Model (Phase 1 Version)

A simple score can help prioritize follow-up.

### Example Scoring

#### +2 points each
- business revenue at a strong fit level
- interest in S-Corp strategy
- interest in tax planning or CFO support
- industry fit: construction or restaurant
- already has bookkeeping complexity

#### +1 point each
- referral source
- clear pain point stated
- urgent timeline
- completed full intake

#### -2 points each
- only wants cheapest filing
- unclear or invalid business
- low strategic fit

### Suggested Priority Bands
- **High Priority:** 5+
- **Medium Priority:** 3–4
- **Low Priority:** 0–2
- **Disqualify:** negative fit or clear mismatch

## Sales Follow-Up Standards

## Booking Follow-Up
If a qualified lead does not book:
- day 0: acknowledgement + booking prompt
- day 2: reminder
- day 5: value-based follow-up
- day 10: final prompt before nurture

## Proposal Follow-Up
After proposal sent:
- day 2: confirmation / questions
- day 5: follow-up
- day 10: objection handling / next step
- day 21: move to nurture or closed lost if no traction

## No-Show Handling
- same-day reschedule message
- 24-hour reminder to rebook
- final follow-up after several days
- move to nurture if no response

## Role of the Pipeline in the Larger System

This pipeline is the operating bridge between:

- website CTA system
- assessment / intake forms
- booking flow
- sales conversations
- proposal delivery
- onboarding handoff

It should be treated as the source of truth for lead movement.

If the website says the primary CTA is a strategy call and the funnel says the primary journey is assessment to booked call, the CRM must reinforce that same logic at every stage.

---

## Phase 1 Implementation Notes

For the first implementation, keep the pipeline simple and consistent.

Do not overcomplicate with dozens of stages or deep branching.
The main requirement is that every lead has:

- a source
- a status
- an owner
- a next action
- a likely offer path
- a clear outcome

That is enough to support launch.
