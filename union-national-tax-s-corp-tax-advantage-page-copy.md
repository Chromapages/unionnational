# Section 1 — Problem / Agitation

## Headline
Many business owners are paying more tax than they need to — simply because their structure has not kept up with their business.

## Body Copy
A lot of self-employed professionals and growing small business owners start as sole proprietors or single-member LLCs and stay there far too long.

That is not always a problem in the beginning. But as profit grows, the wrong structure can quietly create avoidable tax drag.

You may be working hard, generating solid income, and still losing more to self-employment tax than necessary — not because you did anything wrong, but because no one helped you reevaluate the structure as your business matured.

That is where strategic review matters.

The right S-Corp setup can create meaningful tax efficiency for the right business. The wrong setup, or a rushed setup, can create confusion, payroll issues, compliance mistakes, and bad advice.

Union National Tax helps you evaluate the opportunity the right way — based on your numbers, your role in the business, and your broader financial strategy.

# Section 4 — Who It Is Not For

## Headline
When an S-Corp may not be the right move

## Body Copy
Not every business should become an S-Corp.

In some cases, the extra compliance, payroll requirements, or administrative overhead may outweigh the benefit. In other cases, the business may simply not be at the right profit level yet.

That is why the first step should be evaluation, not assumption.

## Not Ideal Bullets
- Very early businesses with limited or unstable profit
- Owners looking for a shortcut without ongoing compliance discipline
- Businesses that are not ready to run payroll properly if the structure requires it
- Anyone expecting the same strategy to work for every business regardless of context

## Reassurance Copy
The goal is not to push every business into an S-Corp.
The goal is to help the right businesses make the right move at the right time.

# Section 9 — FAQ Section

## FAQ 1
### How do I know if an S-Corp is right for me?
The right answer depends on your business profit, the role you play in the company, how you pay yourself, and how the business is currently structured. That is why review comes first.

## FAQ 2
### Is an S-Corp always the best tax strategy?
No. It can be powerful in the right scenario, but it is not the right fit for every business. The decision should be based on actual business conditions, not internet hype.

## FAQ 3
### Do I need to already have an LLC?
Not always. Your current setup will affect the next steps, but the right path depends on how your business is presently structured and what changes are needed.

## FAQ 4
### Does this only help with filing Form 2553?
No. Filing is only one part of the picture. The real value comes from assessing whether the strategy fits, implementing it correctly, and supporting the business afterward.

## FAQ 5
### What happens after I book a call?
You will speak with Union National Tax about your business, current structure, goals, and whether an S-Corp review makes sense as the next step.

## FAQ 6
### Can this connect to bookkeeping or CFO support later?
Yes. For many businesses, S-Corp strategy is the starting point that leads into stronger planning, cleaner reporting, and more ongoing financial support.
